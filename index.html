Stride Lab

Running form analysis from any camera angle — upload a clip, get joint-level flags and drills. Everything runs in your browser; your video never leaves your device.

How to film for good results

  1. Front, back, or side view — all work. The app auto-detects the angle and measures what that view can see. Head-on or from behind: hip drop, knee valgus, crossover, arm swing. From the side: overstriding, trunk lean, knee bend at landing, vertical bounce. Cadence works from any angle.
  2. Treadmill is ideal — prop the phone at roughly hip height, 10–15 ft away. Straight-on or perpendicular beats an oblique angle (angled clips still work, but angle-based numbers get approximate).
  3. Whole body in frame, 10–20 s of steady running. Good lighting and fitted clothing help tracking. (Only the first 30 s are analyzed.)
